# Quantitative Decision Lab

## Quantitative Risk & Decision Intelligence Platform

Quantitative Decision Lab is an end-to-end quantitative analytics project designed to model risk, uncertainty and decision-making in complex financial and industrial systems.

The project combines financial data engineering, statistical modelling, risk analytics, Monte Carlo simulation, optimisation and interactive dashboarding to support better decisions under uncertainty.

## Project Objective

The goal of this project is to build a professional-grade quantitative decision platform that can:

* collect, clean and structure financial and business-related data;
* analyse asset behaviour, volatility, correlations and risk factors;
* compute portfolio risk and performance metrics;
* simulate uncertain scenarios using Monte Carlo methods;
* evaluate decisions under different market and business conditions;
* optimise allocations, strategies or resource decisions;
* present results through an interactive dashboard and consulting-style reports.

## Motivation

Modern companies and financial institutions operate under uncertainty. Market conditions, interest rates, supply chains, demand, costs and operational constraints can all affect strategic decisions.

This project aims to apply engineering, statistics, data science and quantitative finance to build structured tools for risk analysis and decision support.

The project is designed as a professional portfolio piece at the intersection of:

* Data Science
* Quantitative Finance
* Risk Analytics
* Decision Science
* Operations Research
* Systems Engineering
* Technical Consulting
* High-Performance Computing

## Planned Modules

### 1. Market Data Pipeline

Financial data ingestion, cleaning and transformation.

This module will collect historical market data for equities, ETFs, bonds and commodities, and prepare it for analysis.

### 2. Cross-Asset Risk Analytics

Computation of key financial and statistical indicators, including:

* returns;
* volatility;
* drawdowns;
* Sharpe ratio;
* Sortino ratio;
* beta;
* correlation matrices;
* Value at Risk;
* Conditional Value at Risk.

### 3. Scenario Analysis & Stress Testing

Evaluation of how portfolios or decisions behave under adverse scenarios, such as:

* market crashes;
* interest rate shocks;
* energy price increases;
* sector-specific downturns;
* volatility spikes.

### 4. Monte Carlo Simulation

Simulation of uncertain future outcomes using stochastic methods.

This module will be used to estimate distributions of possible results instead of relying on a single deterministic forecast.

### 5. Decision Optimisation

Optimisation of decisions under uncertainty.

Possible applications include:

* portfolio allocation;
* risk-adjusted asset selection;
* capital allocation;
* resource allocation;
* supply chain or aerospace-inspired decision problems.

### 6. Strategy Backtesting

Testing and comparison of systematic decision rules, such as:

* momentum strategies;
* mean reversion strategies;
* risk parity allocation;
* volatility-based allocation;
* defensive allocation rules.

The objective is not only to find profitable strategies, but to understand risk, robustness and decision quality.

### 7. Performance Optimisation

Benchmarking and optimisation of computationally intensive modules using:

* vectorisation;
* NumPy optimisation;
* Numba;
* multiprocessing;
* potentially GPU acceleration.

### 8. Dashboard

Interactive dashboard for visualising:

* portfolio performance;
* risk metrics;
* scenario results;
* Monte Carlo simulations;
* optimisation outputs;
* decision recommendations.
